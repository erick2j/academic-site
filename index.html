About Me

I am a second-year Ph.D student at Boston University advised by Edward Chien. I am interested in Computer Graphics, more specifically the field of Geometry Processing. I want to find new ways to use discrete differential geometry and geometric measure theory to solve interesting problems in Computer Graphics. Before coming to Boston, I earned my B.Sc in Applied and Computational Mathematics at Caltech where I was advised by Peter Schröder. I am grateful to have been supported by a Questbridge National College Match Scholarship during my time there.
